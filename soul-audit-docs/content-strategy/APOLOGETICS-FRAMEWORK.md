# Apologetics Framework

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 16, 2026
**Purpose:** Define apologetics content areas for EUONGELION

---

## Core Principle

Apologetics content must be:

- **Respectful:** Present other views fairly, not as strawmen
- **Rigorous:** Use scholarly sources, cite everything
- **Relational:** Remember the person behind the question
- **Christ-centered:** Point to Jesus, not just "winning arguments"

## Six Apologetics Tracks

### 1. Christianity & Islam

**Core Questions:**

- How do Christians and Muslims view Jesus differently?
- What does the Quran say about Christ vs. what the Bible says?
- How do we understand Muhammad's claims about Jesus?
- Can the Quran and Bible both be true?
- How do Christians engage Muslims with love and truth?

**Key Topics:**
| Topic | Christian View | Islamic View | Key Difference |
|-------|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| Jesus | Fully God, fully man | Prophet, not divine | Deity of Christ |
| Crucifixion | Historical fact, atoning sacrifice | Did not happen (Quran 4:157) | Central event of salvation |
| Trinity | One God in three persons | Shirk (polytheism) | Nature of God |
| Scripture | Bible preserved, inspired | Bible corrupted, Quran final | Authority |
| Salvation | By grace through faith | By works and Allah's will | Assurance |

**Recommended Series: "Jesus According to Islam and Christianity" (14 days)**

1. Who Muslims say Jesus was
2. Who Christians say Jesus was
3. The Quran on Jesus: Surprising agreements
4. The Quran on Jesus: Critical differences
5. Did Jesus really die on the cross? (Historical evidence)
6. The Trinity: Misunderstood by both
7. Muhammad's view of the Gospel
8. What the earliest sources say about Jesus
9. Salvation: Grace vs. works
10. Common ground: Shared values
11. Engaging Muslims with love
12. Testimonies: Muslims who met Jesus
13. Hard questions Muslims ask Christians
14. Hard questions Christians ask Muslims

**Key Resources:**

- Nabeel Qureshi, _Seeking Allah, Finding Jesus_
- James White, _What Every Christian Needs to Know About the Quran_
- David Wood / Acts17Apologetics (YouTube)
- Jay Smith (historical apologetics)

### 2. Christianity & Judaism

**Core Questions:**

- Why do Jews still exist if Jesus was the Messiah?
- Why don't Jewish people accept Jesus as Messiah?
- Why do many Jews prioritize rabbinic tradition over Scripture?
- What is the relationship between Old and New Testaments?
- Is Christianity anti-Semitic?

**Key Topics:**
| Topic | Christian View | Jewish View | Key Issue |
|-------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Jesus | The Messiah promised | Failed/false messiah | Messianic prophecy interpretation |
| Torah | Fulfilled in Christ | Eternal, central | Continuity vs. fulfillment |
| Oral Law (Talmud) | Not authoritative | Equally authoritative | Source of authority |
| Salvation | Through Messiah's atonement | Through Torah observance | Mechanism of atonement |
| Covenant | New covenant includes Gentiles | Eternal covenant with Israel | Covenant theology |

**Recommended Series: "The Jewish Roots of Christianity" (21 days)**

**Week 1: Understanding Judaism**

1. What is Judaism today? (Orthodox, Conservative, Reform, Messianic)
2. Torah: What Jewish people believe about Scripture
3. Talmud: Why rabbinic tradition matters to Jews
4. The Oral Law: Where did it come from?
5. Why the word of rabbis over the word of God?
6. The synagogue: How Jews worship
7. Jewish holidays and their meaning

**Week 2: The Messianic Question** 8. What Jews expect from Messiah 9. Why Jesus doesn't fit (Jewish objections) 10. Messianic prophecies: Isaiah 53 11. Messianic prophecies: Daniel 9 12. Messianic prophecies: Psalm 22 13. The suffering servant vs. conquering king 14. "He came to his own, and his own received him not"

**Week 3: Christianity's Jewish Identity** 15. Jesus the Jew: Rabbi, prophet, messiah 16. The apostles: All Jewish 17. The early church: Entirely Jewish 18. Why most Jews rejected Jesus then 19. Why most Jews reject Jesus now 20. Messianic Judaism: Jews who believe 21. How Christians should relate to Jewish people

**Key Resources:**

- Michael L. Brown, _Answering Jewish Objections to Jesus_ (5 volumes)
- Arnold Fruchtenbaum, _Israelology_ and _Messianic Christology_
- One for Israel Ministry (YouTube/website)
- Jews for Jesus resources

### 3. Christianity & Atheism

**Core Questions:**

- Does God exist? (Arguments for theism)
- Why is there something rather than nothing?
- Can morality exist without God?
- What about evolution?
- Why does God allow suffering?

**Key Topics:**
| Argument | Summary | Key Proponent |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Cosmological | Universe had a beginning; needs a cause | William Lane Craig |
| Teleological | Design implies a designer | William Dembski |
| Moral | Objective morality requires God | C.S. Lewis |
| Ontological | Greatest conceivable being must exist | Alvin Plantinga |
| Experiential | Religious experience as evidence | Alister McGrath |

**Recommended Series: "Why Believe?" (21 days)**

**Week 1: Does God Exist?**

1. The question that won't go away
2. Something from nothing? The cosmological argument
3. Fine-tuned for life: The teleological argument
4. Where does morality come from?
5. The argument from consciousness
6. The argument from reason (why trust our minds?)
7. Responding to "Who made God?"

**Week 2: The Problem of Evil and Suffering** 8. If God is good, why is there evil? 9. Types of evil: Natural, moral, demonic 10. Free will and the possibility of love 11. Suffering and the cross 12. The atheist's problem with evil 13. Where is God when it hurts? 14. Testimony: Finding God in suffering

**Week 3: Faith and Reason** 15. Is faith blind? 16. Christianity and the birth of science 17. Evolution: What it does and doesn't explain 18. Miracles: Can they happen? 19. The resurrection: Best explanation of the evidence 20. Why the New Atheists get it wrong 21. Invitation: Come and see

**Key Resources:**

- William Lane Craig, _Reasonable Faith_
- C.S. Lewis, _Mere Christianity_
- Alister McGrath, _The Dawkins Delusion_
- Tim Keller, _The Reason for God_
- John Lennox, _God's Undertaker_

### 5. Historicity of Scripture

- Can we trust the Bible?
- How do we know the Gospels are reliable?
- Did Jesus really exist?
- Has the Bible been changed over time?
- What about contradictions?

**Key Topics:**
| Topic | Evidence | Significance |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| Manuscript evidence | 5,800+ Greek NT manuscripts | More than any ancient text |
| Eyewitness testimony | Written within eyewitness lifetimes | Too early for legend |
| Archaeological confirmation | Hundreds of verified details | Historically grounded |
| Early church citations | 36,000+ patristic quotes | Could reconstruct NT |
| Hostile witnesses | Josephus, Tacitus, Pliny | Non-Christian confirmation |

**Recommended Series: "Can We Trust the Bible?" (14 days)**

**Week 1: The Text Itself**

1. How we got the Bible (overview)
2. The manuscript evidence
3. How copying worked in the ancient world
4. What about textual variants?
5. The Dead Sea Scrolls and the Old Testament
6. The earliest New Testament manuscripts
7. Has the Bible been corrupted?

**Week 2: Historical Reliability** 8. Did Jesus really exist? (Non-Christian sources) 9. Are the Gospels eyewitness accounts? 10. When were the Gospels written? 11. What about contradictions? 12. Archaeological confirmation 13. Prophecy fulfilled 14. The Bible's unity: 40 authors, one story

## Content Guidelines for Apologetics

### Tone

- **Confident but humble:** We have good reasons, but we hold them graciously
- **Respectful of others:** Present opposing views fairly, not as strawmen
- **Evangelistic:** The goal is relationship with Christ, not winning debates
- **Pastoral:** Remember the person behind the question

### Structure (Following EUONGELION template)

Each apologetics devotional follows the standard structure:

- **Hook:** A modern scenario where this question arises
- **Scripture:** What the Bible says
- **Hebrew/Greek:** Key terms that illuminate
- **Evidence/Argument:** The apologetic content
- **Christ Connection:** How this points to Jesus
- **Reflection:** Questions for the reader
- **Endnotes:** Full citations

### Citation Requirements

- All factual claims need citations
- All quotes need citations
- Historical claims need scholarly backing
- Use primary sources where possible
- Endnote format: superscript number in text, full citation at end

### Review Process

- Apologetics content requires additional theological review
- Claims about other religions must be verified against their primary sources
- Scientific claims must be current and accurately represented
