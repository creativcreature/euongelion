## The Calling

**Shepherd God's flock to Jesus. Find the lost sheep. Help them find their way back home — to their relationship with God.**

## What EUONGELION Is

### For Individuals

A guided path back to seeking God. Honest, immersive devotional content that meets people where they are and walks with them daily.

### For Shepherds (Ministry Leaders)

Functional tools to organize ministry work and create intentional materials — signage, leave-behinds, devotional prints — designed to be _received_ well, not just broadcast.

---

## Core Values

### Honesty

No wild claims. Transparent about what technology does and doesn't do. If it's an algorithm, we call it an algorithm. The Word speaks for itself — we don't oversell.

### Authenticity

Not performative. Not superficial. Every choice should be true, not clever.

### Design as Worship

The Sistine Chapel approach — praising God with the skills He gave. High-brow design brought to democratic material. Servant-hearted excellence.

### Functional Beauty

Good design is functional. Great design is functional AND beautiful. The aesthetic serves immersion, context, clarity, readability — not flexing.

### Word Undiminished

The content is honest, not propaganda — though it may be designed to look like propaganda to flip expectations. We don't water down. We illuminate.

### Games as Teaching

Not gamification for engagement. Games serve understanding — teaching mechanisms, not tricks.

## Language Principles

### Internal vs. Public

| Context                    | Approach                                                     |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Internal (Team/Agents)** | Sleep, Awake, Shepherd — pathway codes for content targeting |
| **Public-Facing**          | No labels. The user should feel _met_, not _sorted_.         |

### Honest Framing

- Say what things are, not what sounds spiritual
- "We try to find content that fits where you are" — not "We hear your soul"
- Technology is a tool, not a pastor
- The Word does the work; we just clear the path

## Success

**A person continues doing devotionals on the site and shares it with their peers.**

This shows:

- They are continuing the walk with God
- They find the material useful toward that end
- The shepherd's reach is extending through them

Not vanity metrics. Evidence of continued seeking.

---

## What We're NOT Doing

- Making wild spiritual claims about technology
- Gamifying for engagement (games teach, not hook)
- Building community features in MVP (pinned for later)
- Using propaganda tactics (though we may subvert the aesthetic)
- Overwhelming users with options
- Forcing frameworks where they don't belong
- Labeling or categorizing users publicly

---

## The Filter

Before any feature, design choice, or piece of content:

1. **Is this honest?**
2. **Does this help someone take a step closer to God?**
3. **Does this diminish the Word or illuminate it?**

If any answer is "no" — we don't build it.
