A Story

In 1972, a Japanese soldier named Hiroo Onoda emerged from the Philippine jungle. He had been hiding for twenty-nine years.

Sent to Lubang Island in 1944 with orders to never surrender, Onoda refused to believe the war had ended. Leaflets announcing Japan's surrender? Propaganda. Family members with loudspeakers? A trick.

It wasn't until his former commanding officer personally issued the order to stand down that Onoda finally surrendered.

He had been free for twenty-nine years. He just didn't know it.

Some of you
have been living
in a war that's
already over.

Fighting. Surviving. Convinced you have to earn God's favor, that you're not good enough, that the battle is still raging.

But the euangelion has already been declared.

"It is finished."

You don't have to keep fighting. You just have to come out of the jungle.

"He restores my soul." Psalm 23:3

That's the word—shuv. He brings your soul back home.

The euangelion makes this possible. You can come home. The door is open. The Father is waiting.

If you've drifted, He's looking for you.

If you've doubted, He can handle your questions.

If you've never believed, the invitation stands.
