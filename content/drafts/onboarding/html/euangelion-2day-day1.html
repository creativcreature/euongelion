The Word

Eu-an-GEL-ee-on (εὐαγγέλιον)

In the ancient world, euangelion wasn't a religious term. It was a military one. When a messenger ran from the battlefield to announce that the war was won—that announcement was euangelion. Good news. The kind that changes everything.

The battle is over. The King has won. Everything is about to change.

A soldier
in the jungle

In 1972, a Japanese soldier named Hiroo Onoda emerged from the Philippine jungle. He had been hiding for twenty-nine years.

Sent to Lubang Island in 1944 with orders to never surrender, Onoda refused to believe the war had ended. Leaflets? Propaganda. Family members with loudspeakers? A trick.

It wasn't until his former commanding officer personally issued the order to stand down that Onoda finally surrendered.

He had been free for twenty-nine years. He just didn't know it.

Some of you have been living in a war that's already over. But the euangelion has already been declared. "It is finished."
