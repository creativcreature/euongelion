How do we live as people who have encountered the Savior the angels announced?

We’ve completed our investigation into Jesus’s identity as the necessary Messiah and ultimate Savior. We’ve examined the evidence, understood the historical context, considered the contemporary implications, and reflected on our response. Today is about integration—taking what we’ve learned and building it into the practical fabric of our daily lives.

Day 6: Review and Practice

“The shepherds returned, glorifying and praising God for all the things they had heard and seen, which were just as they had been told.” - Luke 2:20

Quickstart (1 Minute Commitment)

Today’s Verse: Luke 2:20 - “The shepherds returned, glorifying and praising God for all the things they had heard and seen, which were just as they had been told.”

Greek Word: δοξάζω (Doxazo) - “To glorify” or “to give glory.” This means to recognize and declare someone’s true worth and significance. The shepherds’ response was to live in a way that reflected the magnitude of what they had discovered.

Reflection Question: How can I live in a way that reflects the reality that Jesus is my Savior and Lord? What would change in my daily routine if I truly believed this?

Prayer: “Help me to integrate my understanding of Jesus as Savior into every aspect of my life, so that my actions, relationships, and priorities reflect this foundational truth.”

Story Time (5 Minutes)

Think about a time when you received life-changing news—perhaps a job offer that would transform your career, a medical diagnosis that required major lifestyle changes, or the birth of a child that made you a parent. Initially, the news might have felt surreal, almost too big to fully grasp. But gradually, as the reality sank in, you began to make practical adjustments.

You probably didn’t change everything at once. Instead, you started with the most obvious and necessary adjustments, then gradually worked through the implications for other areas of your life. Some changes were immediate and dramatic; others were subtle and evolved over time. But the news became a new organizing principle that influenced countless decisions, both large and small.

This is similar to what happens when we truly accept that Jesus is our Savior and Lord. The reality is so significant that it can’t be confined to one area of life—it gradually influences everything. Like the shepherds who “returned, glorifying and praising God,” we find ourselves living differently because we’ve encountered something that changes our fundamental understanding of reality.

The shepherds didn’t abandon their work as shepherds, but they returned to it with a new perspective. They had met the Messiah, and that encounter became the lens through which they viewed everything else. Their ordinary work was now part of a larger story about God’s salvation of the world.

Takeaway: Accepting Jesus as Savior doesn’t require abandoning our normal responsibilities, but it does transform how we approach them and why we do them.

Deep Dive (15 Minutes)

Practical Implementation:
Living as Saved People

Full Passage: Luke 2:8-20 (NIV)
8 And there were shepherds living out in the fields nearby, keeping watch over their flocks at night. 9 An angel of the Lord appeared to them, and the glory of the Lord shone around them, and they were terrified. 10 But the angel said to them, “Do not be afraid. I bring you good news that will cause great joy for all the people. 11 Today in the town of David a Savior has been born to you; he is the Messiah, the Lord.”12 This will be a sign to you: You will find a baby wrapped in cloths and lying in a manger.” 13 Suddenly a great company of the heavenly host appeared with the angel, praising God and saying, 14 “Glory to God in the highest heaven, and on earth peace to those on whom his favor rests.”15 When the angels had left them and gone into heaven, the shepherds said to one another, “Let’s go to Bethlehem and see this thing that has happened, which the Lord has told us about.” 16 So they hurried off and found Mary and Joseph, and the baby, who was lying in the manger. 17 When they had seen him, they spread the word concerning what had been told them about this child, 18 and all who heard it were amazed at what the shepherds said to them. 19 But Mary treasured up all these things and pondered them in her heart. 20 The shepherds returned, glorifying and praising God for all the things they had heard and seen, which were just as they had been told.

Developing a Savior-Centered Identity

The first practical step is allowing our identity to be shaped by our relationship with Jesus rather than by our achievements, failures, or social roles. This involves:

Daily Reminder: Beginning each day by remembering that you are loved, forgiven, and accepted by God through Jesus. This provides a foundation of security that doesn’t depend on your performance or circumstances.

Identity Statements: Regularly affirming truths about who you are in Christ: “I am a child of God,” “I am forgiven,” “I am being transformed,” “I have a purpose.” These aren’t just positive thinking but declarations of theological reality.

Perspective on Failure: When you fall short of your own standards, remembering that your worth isn’t based on perfect performance but on God’s grace. This enables you to acknowledge mistakes honestly without being crushed by shame.

Motivation for Growth: Pursuing personal development not to earn God’s approval but as a response to the love and salvation you’ve already received.

Practicing Gratitude and Worship

The shepherds’ response was to glorify and praise God. This can become a regular practice that keeps us grounded in the reality of what God has done:

Daily Gratitude: Ending each day by identifying specific things you’re grateful for, particularly evidences of God’s grace and provision in your life.

Worship Practices: Engaging in activities that acknowledge God’s greatness and goodness—singing, prayer, reading Scripture, spending time in nature, or participating in corporate worship.

Celebration of Ordinary Moments: Learning to see God’s presence and blessing in everyday experiences, not just dramatic events.

Sharing Your Story: Like the shepherds who “spread the word,” finding appropriate ways to share how your encounter with Jesus has affected your life.

Aligning Priorities with Kingdom Values

Jesus’s lordship means allowing his values and priorities to shape our decisions:

Time Management: Evaluating how you spend your time in light of what Jesus said was most important—loving God and loving others. This might mean adjusting your schedule to include more time for relationships, service, or spiritual growth.

Financial Decisions: Viewing your resources as gifts from God to be used for his purposes, not just personal comfort or security. This could involve more generous giving, simpler living, or more thoughtful consumption.

Career Choices: Considering how your work can serve God’s purposes and benefit others, not just advance your own interests. This doesn’t necessarily mean changing careers, but it might mean changing how you approach your current work.

Relationship Priorities: Investing more deeply in relationships that matter most and approaching all relationships with greater love, forgiveness, and service.

Pursuing Transformation

Accepting Jesus as Savior begins a process of transformation that continues throughout life:

Character Development: Actively working to develop the character qualities Jesus modeled—love, patience, kindness, humility, integrity, courage. This involves both spiritual practices and practical choices.

Breaking Destructive Patterns: Identifying habits, attitudes, or behaviors that are inconsistent with your new identity in Christ and taking concrete steps to change them.

Spiritual Disciplines: Developing regular practices that nurture your relationship with God—prayer, Bible study, meditation, fasting, solitude, service.

Community Involvement: Connecting with other believers for mutual encouragement, accountability, and growth. This might involve joining a church, small group, or other Christian community.

Serving Others

The angels announced that Jesus’s birth was “good news for all the people.” Living as saved people means participating in God’s mission to bless others:

Acts of Service: Looking for practical ways to help people in your community, workplace, or family. This could be as simple as helping a neighbor or as involved as volunteering with a charitable organization.

Justice and Advocacy: Standing up for those who are marginalized, oppressed, or voiceless. Jesus consistently showed concern for the poor, the outcast, and the powerless.

Sharing the Gospel: Finding natural and appropriate ways to share the good news about Jesus with others who might be interested. This doesn’t require becoming a professional evangelist, but it does mean being open about your faith when opportunities arise.

Hospitality and Welcome: Creating spaces where people feel valued, accepted, and loved, regardless of their background or beliefs.

Dealing with Challenges

Living as a follower of Jesus in a complex world involves ongoing challenges:

Handling Doubt: Recognizing that questions and uncertainties are normal parts of faith development. Continue seeking answers while acting on what you do believe.

Managing Failure: When you fall short of your own standards or Jesus’s teachings, quickly acknowledging your mistakes, seeking forgiveness, and recommitting to growth.

Navigating Cultural Tensions: Learning to live faithfully in a pluralistic society while showing respect and love for people who hold different beliefs.

Persevering Through Difficulty: Remembering that following Jesus doesn’t eliminate suffering but provides meaning, hope, and support in the midst of challenges.

Measuring Progress

How do we know if we’re successfully integrating our faith into daily life? Some indicators include:

Increased Peace: Growing sense of security and contentment that doesn’t depend on external circumstances.

Greater Love: More patience, kindness, and compassion in your relationships with others.

Clearer Purpose: Better understanding of why you’re here and how your life fits into God’s larger purposes.

Moral Growth: Progress in areas where you previously struggled with temptation or character issues.

Deeper Joy: Not just happiness based on circumstances, but a deeper satisfaction that comes from knowing you’re living according to your true purpose.

Optional Journal Reflection

  1. What specific changes will you make this week to better align your daily life with your understanding of Jesus as Savior and Lord?

  2. Which areas of your life—work, relationships, finances, time management—most need to be influenced by your faith? What would that look like practically?

  3. How will you maintain the insights and commitments you’ve made during this week’s study? What practices will help you continue growing in your understanding and application of the Gospel?

Share Your Thoughts
