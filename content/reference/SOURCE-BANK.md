## PART 1: BY THEME

### Grace & Salvation

**Augustine of Hippo (354-430 AD)**
> "You have made us for yourself, O Lord, and our hearts are restless until they rest in you."

*Citation:* Augustine, *Confessions*, trans. Henry Chadwick (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1991), Book I.1.

---

> "Grace is given not because we have done good works, but in order that we may be able to do them."

*Citation:* Augustine, *On Grace and Free Will*, Chapter 17.

---

**Martin Luther (1483-1546)**
> "The law says, 'Do this,' and it is never done. Grace says, 'Believe in this,' and everything is already done."

*Citation:* Martin Luther, *Preface to the Letter of St. Paul to the Romans* (1522), in Luther's Works, vol. 35.

---

> "God does not save those who are only imaginary sinners. Be a sinner, and let your sins be strong, but let your trust in Christ be stronger."

*Citation:* Martin Luther, Letter to Philip Melanchthon, August 1, 1521, in Luther's Works, vol. 48, p. 281-282.

---

**Dietrich Bonhoeffer (1906-1945)**
> "Cheap grace is grace without discipleship, grace without the cross, grace without Jesus Christ, living and incarnate."

*Citation:* Dietrich Bonhoeffer, *The Cost of Discipleship*, trans. R.H. Fuller (New York: Touchstone, 1995), 44.

---

> "Costly grace is the gospel which must be sought again and again and again, the gift which must be asked for, the door at which a man must knock."

*Citation:* Dietrich Bonhoeffer, *The Cost of Discipleship*, trans. R.H. Fuller (New York: Touchstone, 1995), 45.

---

**C.S. Lewis (1898-1963)**
> "I believe in Christianity as I believe that the sun has risen: not only because I see it, but because by it I see everything else."

*Citation:* C.S. Lewis, "Is Theology Poetry?" (1944), in *The Weight of Glory and Other Addresses* (New York: HarperOne, 2001), 140.
