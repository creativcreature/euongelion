## The Core Problem: Why People Can't Engage

### What Reddit/Forums Reveal

The **real struggles** (not the polished church answers):

| Barrier               | What People Actually Say                                                         | Implication for EUONGELION                       |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **ADHD/Focus**        | "My brain has a hard time focusing... the Bible can feel like a 50-pound weight" | Build for scattered attention, not concentration |
| **Guilt Spiral**      | "I feel guilty if I don't do my devotions... then I avoid it more"               | Remove guilt, reframe the relationship           |
| **Digital Addiction** | "I can scroll TikTok for hours but can't read one verse"                         | Make it as effortless as scrolling               |
| **Stress Addiction**  | "I feel anxious when I'm not being productive"                                   | Rest as radical, not lazy                        |
| **Burnout**           | "I'm too tired to think about anything deep"                                     | Low cognitive load, high impact                  |
| **Boredom**           | "It feels repetitive... I don't understand what I'm reading"                     | Context, story, fresh angles                     |
| **Comparison**        | "Other Christians have it figured out and I don't"                               | Normalize struggle                               |

### The Hustle Culture Trap

Research shows:

- "Being addicted to stress isn't just a mindset—it's a **biochemical loop** your brain gets stuck in" ([Zencare](https://blog.zencare.co/stress-addiction-hustle-culture-burnout/))
- "Productivity has become a moral compass... rest feels like a sin" ([Medium](https://medium.com/christian-futurism/your-hustle-culture-is-destroying-your-soul-38fd1b6b8427))
- 62% of users experience digital burnout ([Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/anxiety-in-high-achievers/202505/how-to-rise-above-hustle-culture))

**This explains why people can't slow down:**
They're chemically wired for constant motion. Stillness feels dangerous. Rest triggers guilt. Their identity is fused with output.

## What Devotionals Get Wrong

| Common Problem             | What People Say                                                             | EUONGELION Opportunity              |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Guilt-based**            | "You wake up and know if you don't do your devotions, you will feel guilty" | Permission-based, not obligation    |
| **Oversimplified**         | "Just pray more" doesn't help anxiety                                       | Honest about complexity             |
| **Performance-driven**     | "I feel like I'm earning God's approval"                                    | Grace-centered, not streak-centered |
| **One-size-fits-all**      | "Quiet time" format doesn't fit everyone                                    | Multiple entry points (1/5/15 min)  |
| **Ignores neurodiversity** | "ADHD makes these repetitive tasks hard"                                    | Built for distracted minds          |
| **Avoids real pain**       | Churches "marginalize" mental health struggles                              | Directly addresses suffering        |
| **Churchy language**       | Jargon alienates seekers                                                    | Honest, human language              |

## Deconstruction/Exvangelical Insights

### Why People Leave (and what they're NOT finding)

From research on r/Deconstruction (~10K members) and exvangelical communities:

| Reason for Leaving       | What They Needed But Didn't Find            |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| Questions treated as sin | Space for honest doubt                      |
| Church hurt              | Acknowledgment of pain caused by Christians |
| LGBTQ exclusion          | Love without conditions                     |
| Political fusion         | Faith separate from partisan identity       |
| Burnout from service     | Rest without guilt                          |
| Oversimplified answers   | Intellectual honesty                        |
| Patriarchy/abuse         | Safety and equality                         |

**42% of adults have deconstructed the faith of their youth** — including 37% of current churchgoing Christians ([Barna](https://www.barna.com/trends/ex-christians-deconstructing/)).

**EUONGELION Opportunity:** Many who deconstructed didn't leave God—they left a _version_ of God worth leaving. Reconstruction is trending.
